01 — Principles

Three commitments, held in tension.

Lumen sits between two impulses: the precision of an engineering tool and the warmth of an object you'd want on a desk. Every choice is calibrated against these three principles.

P · 01

Structured, not stiff

A 4-pixel grid. Strict typographic scale. Predictable spacing. The skeleton is rigid so the surface can be generous — softness is earned through structure, not added in spite of it.

P · 02

Luminous, not loud

One blue, used with intent. Subtle gradients where flatness would feel cold. Highlights that suggest the surface is illuminated from within — never a shout, always a glow.

P · 03

Honest, not literal

The hemispheric forms, chrome details, and gel surfaces are gestures — not reproductions. We reference an era of optimism without dressing up as it.

02 — Color

A spectrum of blues, anchored in honest greys.

Aqua is the single voice of brand and action. Neutrals do everything else — backgrounds, text, structure — and earn their depth from how they layer, not from saturation.

06 — When things go wrong

A grammar for failure.

Most interfaces treat errors as exceptions — bolted-on warnings in red. Lumen treats them as a first-class voice of the system: structured, hierarchical, and always paired with a way out. Four severities, four densities, one consistent tone.

P · 04

Errors are conversations, not warnings

Every error answers three questions in order: what happened, what it means, what you can do next. If we can't answer all three, we shouldn't surface the error to the user. Red is reserved for failures the user can act on — never for ambient noise.

Destructive Confirmation

Used only when an action is irreversible. The pattern is deliberately heavier than other modals so it can't be dismissed by reflex.
RULES · The destructive action is on the right.
· Cancel is the default focus.
· The body names the consequence in plain language.
· No "Are you sure?" — name the thing being lost.

✗ Not Lumen
  • "Error: Save failed."
  • "Invalid password."
  • "Are you sure? This cannot be undone."
  • "Something went wrong. Please try again later."

08 — Motion

Motion that settles.

The system uses a small number of carefully tuned curves. Most transitions are short and ease-out — the interface arrives, it doesn't perform.

Quick · 140ms
cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0.2, 1)Hover, focus, button states. Never noticed, always felt.
Smooth · 240ms
cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)Tab switches, popover reveals, lesson transitions.
Settle · 380ms
cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)Toggle handles, modal entries — a small overshoot, then rest.
Glow · 600ms
cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.6, 1)Reserved for moments of accomplishment — a lesson complete, a streak earned.
